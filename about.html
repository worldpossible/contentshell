What is RACHEL?

RACHEL (Remote Area Community Hotspot for Education and Learning) is a portable, offline education server that brings the world's best free educational content to communities without reliable internet access. RACHEL devices create a local WiFi hotspot that students, teachers, and community members can connect to with any device—smartphones, tablets, laptops, or desktop computers—to access a curated library of educational resources.

RACHEL contains complete offline copies of websites like Wikipedia, Khan Academy, OpenStreetMap, educational videos, e-books, health information, and much more. The content is available in multiple languages including English, Spanish, French, Portuguese, Arabic, Hindi, and many others, making quality education accessible to learners around the world.

About World Possible

World Possible is a U.S.-based 501(c)(3) non-profit organization founded with the mission of connecting offline learners to the world's free educational knowledge. World Possible develops and distributes RACHEL devices to schools, libraries, community centers, refugee camps, prisons, and other locations where internet connectivity is limited, unreliable, or unaffordable.

Since its founding, World Possible has deployed RACHEL devices in over 90 countries, reaching millions of learners. The organization works with partner NGOs, governments, educational institutions, and volunteers to ensure that every learner, regardless of their location or economic circumstances, can access quality educational resources.

World Possible also provides training for teachers and administrators on how to effectively integrate RACHEL into their educational programs, maximizing the impact of offline learning resources.

Datapost: Offline Data Collection

Datapost is a feature integrated into RACHEL that enables offline data collection and synchronization. In environments without internet connectivity, gathering feedback, conducting surveys, tracking student progress, and collecting usage statistics can be challenging. Datapost solves this by allowing data to be collected locally on the RACHEL device and then synchronized when connectivity becomes available.

Key features of Datapost include:

  • Offline Forms: Create and distribute surveys, assessments, and feedback forms that work without internet
  • Usage Analytics: Track which educational modules are being accessed and how often
  • Student Progress: Monitor learning outcomes and engagement metrics
  • Sync When Connected: Automatically upload collected data when the device connects to the internet
  • Webmail Integration: Local email system for communication within the RACHEL network

RACHEL Hardware

RACHEL runs on various hardware platforms designed for different deployment scenarios:

  • RACHEL-Plus: A ruggedized, battery-powered device ideal for mobile deployments and areas with unreliable electricity
  • RACHEL-Pi: A cost-effective solution built on Raspberry Pi hardware, perfect for permanent installations
  • USB RACHEL: A portable USB drive version that can run on any computer
  • Custom Servers: Larger installations using standard server hardware for schools and institutions

All RACHEL devices run on Ubuntu Linux and use open-source software, making them easy to maintain, update, and customize for local needs.

Educational Content

RACHEL includes a growing library of free educational content modules covering:

  • Core Academics: Mathematics, science, reading, and language arts from Khan Academy, CK-12, and other sources
  • Reference: Complete offline Wikipedia, dictionaries, and encyclopedias
  • Health: Medical information, first aid, maternal health, and disease prevention resources
  • Practical Skills: Agriculture, construction, business, and vocational training materials
  • Literature: Thousands of free e-books from Project Gutenberg and other sources
  • Maps: Offline OpenStreetMap for geography and navigation
  • Videos: Educational video content on science, history, and more
